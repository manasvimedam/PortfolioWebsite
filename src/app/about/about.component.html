About Me

Hey there! I'm a second-year student at Rutgers University-New Brunswick from Jackson, New Jersey, double majoring in Computer Science and Finance.

My introduction to computer science was in my 8th-grade computer programming class. I still vividly remember how I sat in front of my computer for hours trying to get the little turtle in Terrapin Logo to write "Hello World". It did not take me long to learn to love the frustration and fulfillment that came with programming. I became obsessed with coding for anything and anyone. My passion lies in finding a way to use programming to solve any and every problem.

Finance is a major that I chose after a lot of consideration. Initially, I wanted a business-related major to advance my career into more than software engineering. However, my time exploring at Rutgers Business School(RBS) has taught me that Finance more than numbers. Finance will give me the ability to have better insight into what the companies and individuals need. With this unique skill set, I know that I can solve any and every problem.

Outside of Computer Science and Finance, you can find me working with a fantastic group of people to empower women in STEM and leadership roles through Rutgers WiCS (Women in Computer Science and RBS's Women BUILD (Business Undergraduates Leadership Development (phew, that's a mouthful)), mentoring incoming underclassmen, teaching my classmates who are struggling in class, drawing my favorite musicians, actors, and actresses, or learning Korean.
